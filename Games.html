    • Analyzing Nintendo Switch game sales

  • I. Introduction

    The Switch is Nintendo’s current flagship gaming console and an extremely popular gaming platform for people worldwide. One of my favorite websites, VGChartz, reports data for game sales across all platforms on a quarterly basis. I wanted to analyze game sales for the Nintendo Switch specifically and see what type of games sold best and at what times; this information would be useful to entities such as retail stores and independent game shops on how to best stock their inventory in preparation for sales. For this dataset, I used ParseHub (an online web scraper based off of APIs) to first populate a Microsoft Excel spreadsheet. I then cleaned the data in R using its cleaning functions found in the lubridate package, from which I converted it to a .CSV file for analysis using SQL queries in PostgreSQL/DBeaver. Finally, I made data visualizations using Google Sheets’ built-in chart tools.

    II. Data Cleaning

    Raw data for this analysis report was scraped directly from VGChartz’s Nintendo Switch game sales section which was directly organized and separated by genre. All 20 data tables, one for each genre, were combined in R to form one comprehensive .CSV file composed of 1979 rows that detailed physical game sales for Switch games. For a more detailed record of all cleaning steps, including snippets of R code, view the log here.

    III. Data Analysis and Visualizations

    Data analysis was conducted by a series of SQL statements executed using the data table loaded into PostgreSQL with DBeaver as the frontend. For a detailed log of all SQL statements written to explore the data, view the query log here.

    The best-selling games by genre of all time were Action, followed by Platform, Sports, Role-Playing, and Music games. Action games sold at 7.3 million while the next highest figures were 5.52, 3.99, 3.33, and 1.93 million respectively. Action games are seen as the most profitably and easy to sell, appealing to a wide variety of gamers who focus on having fun.

    The most numerous developers within Switch games included SNK Corporation, Square Enix, Capcom, and Nintendo at the top. These developers are responsible for the creating the games from start to end and are a reliable source of new games released every year.

    Within the dataset, the top selling games were not necessarily the most numerous. Action was both the top-selling and most numerous genre, but there were more Role-Playing and Adventure games in the dataset even though they sold less than Sports and Platform games. This would be helpful for game developers to look at and create the right genre of games in order to maximize profits for stakeholders. There were less Sports and Platform type games but they still sold more than Role-Playing and Adventure games.

    As expected, most games are released in Q4 which is defined as the months October, November, and December which is commonly known as the holiday season. It makes sense games usually come out during this time because shoppers tend to spend more money during the holidays, indicating that it would help to maximize profit if games were stocked adequately during this time.

    Most games for the Switch came out in 2018 which is fairly early in the console’s lifespan. This was a peak year for games to be made and sold for the platform, as the console just came out and developers were trying to capitalize on its growing prominence. A steady decline in games released over the years indicates that the Switch is approaching the end of its lifespan, to be replaced with a new platform.

    III. Conclusion

    Video game manufacturers, developers, and retail storefronts would find the information here helpful on what type of games to create or sell, as well as when to sell games to maximize profit according to consumer needs.

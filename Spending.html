Introduction

As a recent college grad trying make sense of my finances, I started this project to analyze my past spending history. I want to see exactly what I use my money on and how I can better manage it. After bills, what do I spend my disposable income on and where should I cut back on to save money? Analyzing my history over the past six months, when I finally moved out of my parents house and started to support myself, will allow me to create a budget that accounts for everything

I downloaded my financial activity directly from my bank, University Federal Credit Union, which includes all data since I opened my account. The time period starts on Jan 17th, 2021 to Feb 2nd, 2022 which is roughly one year spending to analyze. The raw data is organized into 162 rows with 4 columns as a .CSV file and is free to access for personal use.

Data cleaning and processing was accomplished in Microsoft Excel and I documented the entire process which can be seen here. Details about what each category is defined as in this dataset are included. I used R to conduct data analysis and create visualizations to give the data meaning.

Starting with the data frame assignment, several columns are visibly in the wrong data type. To fix this, I made a new data frame with the correct types as well as a Boolean variable for if the transaction was a spending one. Spending transactions are explicitly expenses on my part and include everything but Deposit, Income, and Verification. Deposit was for the one-time $25 deposit I had to make to open the account, Verification is for online services to deposit and withdraw a small amount, and Income is money I made from working. The month and year were also extracted and both Value and Balance were converted from strings to numbers.

new_spending <- Spending %>% mutate(is_expense = if_else(Category == "Deposit" | Category == "Verification" | Category == "Income", FALSE, TRUE)) %>% mutate(correct_date = as_date(mdy(Date, tz = NULL, format = NULL))) %>% mutate(month = month(correct_date, label = TRUE)) %>% mutate(year = year(correct_date)) %>% mutate(correct_value = as.numeric(gsub(",","", Value))) %>% mutate(correct_balance = as.numeric(gsub(",","", Balance)))

Conclusion

These analyses about my financial activity show me where and how Im spending my money; although I already consider myself a frugal and prudent person when it comes to finances, it still helps to see areas I can focus on reducing to save money. Necessary expenses such as housing, groceries, medication, and transportation costs will be always prioritized over the luxuries like restaurants, clothes, concert tickets, movies, outings, and video games where I should be more conservative in the future Such luxuries can only be considered after Ive used my money for necessary expenses and even then I need to ensure any budget I create based off of my existing habits can accommodate for those items. This project gave me insight on my behavior and will definitely inform future spending activity.
